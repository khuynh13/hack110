FAQs

What is a hackathon? What's so special about HACK110?

An overnight "marathon" of coding an original project, often accompanied by workshops, mentors, free food, and swag. If you are anticipating continuing in computer science, you'll find that hackathons are a big part of CS culture and networking! Larger hackathons can have industry sponsors, opportunities to interview, contests/prizes, and access to new technologies.

​HACK110 is a hackathon specially designed for those new to the CompSci space! All workshops and projects will be appropriate for the 110 level, and you will have access to TA mentors the whole way.

What is there to do?

-->Hack: work on your own original project through the night with the help of your UTA team

-->Attend workshops: learn the ins and outs of CS culture and cool new development tools

-->Network: talk with rep's from various CS-related clubs and organizations

-->Eat: we will provide dinner, ample nighttime snacks/caffeine, and breakfast

-->Have fun: escape rooms, Smash bros, cup stacking, and much, much more

Will there be food? What if I have dietary restrictions?

Yes! So much food! Everyone will be provided pizza dinner, ample snacks/caffeine throughout the night, and a coffee/donut/bagel breakfast. When you RSVP, please notify us of any dietary restrictions.

How long will it take? Do I have to stay the whole time? ​Can I arrive late?

HACK110 runs from 7pm on 4/12 - 7am on 4/13.

We encourage you to stay as long as you can! However, plenty of people stay only for the workshops in the evening or until their project is complete.

​If you are unable to join us at 7pm, make sure you check in at the help desk so we can get you ramped up to hack!

Can I work in a group? What if I don't know anyone?

Yes! Projects can be submitted in groups of up to (2) people. If you would like to work in a group, there will be a team-forming session for those who need a partner. You are also welcome to work alone if desired (with the support of your TAs :)

Teams can also be formed across sections (just make sure to make note of this when you submit your final project).

What is a 'Demo'? ​Do I have to participate?

"Demos" are the chance to show off your project after a night of hard work. It is low stakes, voluntary (a TA can show it off for you if you'd prefer), and supportive/inclusive as heck! Even if your project is unfinished, we want to see what you have so far!

If you do decide to demo, you will be rewarded with an exclusive "I Demoed at HACK110" sticker!

Can I start early?

NOPE - Hacking starts after you project idea gets approved by a TA and you submit the "Final Project Opt-In" form before midnight on 4/12.

Do I HAVE to attend in order to do a final project instead of ​the problem set

YES - the HACK110 final project option is only for students who attend, get their projects approved by a TA, and submit the "Final Project Opt-In" form.

When is my project due? ​Can I work on it after HACK110 ends?

Projects are not due until LDOC! You can continue working on your project up until this time, but your project should represent 12+ hours worth of work.

What if I want to sleep?

Mattresses and a quiet place to snooze will be provided! Feel free to bring a change of clothes, a toothbrush, and a sleeping bag/blanket/pillow.

What should I bring?

-->Your computer/charger/headphones

​-->Supplies for staying overnight (A change of clothes/toothbrush/sleeping bag/blanket/pillow are all appropriate, but not mandatory)

-->Some general app ideas

-->A positive attitude!

What if I don't know what to create?

Check out some of the past hackathon projects that students just like you have submitted and our list of feasible projects HERE. You don't have to use these suggestions, but if you are thinking of an original idea we recommend talking through your vision with a TA before you get started!

There will also be a team-forming and brainstorming session after the initial workshops!
